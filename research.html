Research & Concept

1. Introduction

The project “Abai & Murakami Digital Heritage” explores how modern web media can bridge cultural and temporal gaps by reimagining classical literature through digital storytelling. It connects the philosophical legacy of Abai Kunanbayev and the surreal imagination of Haruki Murakami, inviting users to experience their ideas interactively through gamification and design.

2. Research Problem

In the 21st century, literature is often overshadowed by fast visual content and digital entertainment. Many young readers find classical works inaccessible or outdated. This project addresses the challenge of re-engaging audiences with classical texts by transforming reading into an immersive digital journey that uses interactivity, visuals, and sound to stimulate curiosity and empathy.

3. Methodology

The website was developed using HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript, supported by Bootstrap 5 for responsive design. The project applies three key media technology approaches:

  • Visual Storytelling: dynamic typography, quote animations, and imagery express emotion and rhythm of both writers.
  • Gamification: interactive elements like quizzes, progress tracking, and star ratings motivate exploration.
  • Digital Humanities Approach: combining literary analysis and UX design to preserve and reinterpret cultural heritage.

4. Comparative Focus: Abai & Murakami

Abai represents moral enlightenment, education, and unity, emphasizing human dignity and the pursuit of wisdom. Murakami embodies modern introspection, surrealism, and emotional complexity. When brought together, they form a dialogue between the Kazakh philosophical tradition and Japanese postmodern storytelling, revealing how wisdom and imagination can coexist in the human search for meaning.

5. Key References

  • Abai Kunanbayev
  • Haruki Murakami

The design approach was inspired by contemporary digital storytelling and gamification concepts used in modern media art and educational technology.

6. Expected Outcome

The project demonstrates that cultural heritage can be not only preserved but also revitalized through interactive design. By merging literature and technology, the website invites users to explore both Kazakh and Japanese philosophical worlds in a playful yet reflective environment — turning reading into an act of discovery.

7. Reflection

Through developing this digital heritage platform, I learned how storytelling, interface design, and cultural studies can merge into a single interactive experience. The process deepened my understanding of how web media can educate, inspire, and preserve the essence of human creativity across generations.
